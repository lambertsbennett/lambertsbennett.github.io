I grew up in Powell River, British Columbia, a beautiful small town on the sunshine coast of Canada. Growing up I was fortunate to have access to both the ocean and the many lakes surrounding Powell River. As a result, I developed a love for the outdoors and, as I grew older, concern for the environment.



From Powell River I moved to Edmonton, Alberta to attend the University of Alberta and get a Bachelor's degree in Civil and Environmental Engineering. This was a formative time for me and solidified my desire to forge a career centred on environmental science and preservation of our environment.

Following my undergraduate studies, I was accepted into the MIT-WHOI Joint Program in Oceanography, where I would work in the labs of Heidi Sosik, Rob Olson, and Roman Stocker.



During my time at the Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution, I worked in the labs of Heidi Sosik and Rob Olson. In this time period I worked on developing a cell sorter for the Imaging FlowCytobot. This was a crash course in embedded systems, electronics, and microcontroller programming for me but, in the end, we successfully constructed a system that was able to sort phytoplankton cells with complex shapes.
